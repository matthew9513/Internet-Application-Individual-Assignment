How do you build a PC?

For starters, you would need the components of a desktop which main component includes the processor, motherboard, RAM, graphics card if for gaming/video editing, storage(SSD & HDD), casing and a power supply. There will be quick links listed down below for easier reference.

1. Picking your components

Many people would ask what parts to choose. Of course, you have to create a budget for the parts that you want to buy first. After that, you can survey at sites like PCPartPicker to get an idea and guide on what to buy for your build. A little tip on picking a power supply is no matter how cheap your budget is, you cannot save for a power supply. It is better to save up some money in order to afford at least a Bronze tier 80+ efficiency power supply since the power supply is the most important part of your PC build. Getting a good power supply also unlocks upgrading capabilities for the prior future.

2. Putting together the components

After waiting for all the parts to be shipped from online or getting the parts from nearby retailers, it is time for the fun part which is assembling your PC. Start by sorting all the parts accordingly starting from motherboard, processor, RAM and m.2 SSD if you bought any. These will be the parts where you will have to assemble first before placing it into the casing.

3. Assembling your processor,RAM and m.2 SSD onto your motherboard

First, go ahead and find the CPU socket on your motherboard. You can't miss it - look for the large square socket somewhere near the middle of your motherboard that has a little metal lever alongside it. After that, you need to first lift up the retention arm to open up the CPU socket on the motherboard and then lift the arm up all the way, and then open up the socket cover. Only Intel CPU sockets have covers - AMD sockets do not. Before picking up your CPU, first look closely at the CPU socket and look for the triangle mark/icon in the bottom left corner. This indicates which way to install your CPU, as there will be an arrow on the CPU itself that goes in this corner. Gently place the processor onto the socket and then lock the CPU socket by lowering the retention arm and clip it back to the hook. All processors will have a heatsink fan included in the packaging and the guide for installing will be right here > Click here. As for the RAM and m.2 SSD, if you are only using 1 RAM stick, look into the motherboard user manual to locate which slot to fit it into. Just unlock the RAM slot by pressing down the small clip located at the edges of the RAM slot, after that just insert the RAM following the shape of the RAM and the slot as there is only one way to fit the RAM in, when the RAM is inserted correctly, there will be a click sound after installing them. For the m.2 SSD if you have one, you can locate the m.2 slot on the motherboard by checking the motherboard user manual if you are not sure, and just insert the m.2 SSD when you found the slot and it is similarly to installing the RAM except you will have to screw the m.2 SSD onto the motherboard.

4. Preparing your desktop casing

Now it is time to prepare your casing before installing everything into a complete PC. Most desktop cases nowadays come with built in fans, but if you bought any extra fans to keep your PC cool, you can install them right now. After that, install your power supply, you can locate the place to install your power supply from the casing user manual but most casings will have the power supply slot at the bottom back of the casing. Now you can start placing the motherboard with all the parts you assembled into the casing. It is best if you put your casing lying flat instead of standing to install your motherboard more easily if you are a beginner. After installing the motherboard, it is time to install your other components for example graphics card and HDD if you bought any. You should also connect the front panel wires to your motherboard by referring to your motherboard user manual. The guide for cable connecting and managing will be linked HERE since it will be too long if it is listed in this page.

5. Starting up your PC and installing Windows

Double check everything is connected to your PC including your monitor, mouse, keyboard and etc before turning your PC on. If everything is installed corrrectly, your PC will boot. For installing windows, follow the guide HERE. Done! Now you have a fully working PC and you can start using it.
